# Give 'n Take Product Roadmap: From Krista to Every Player

## The Strategy in One Sentence

Get this working for Krista first. When she trusts it completely and pours everything she knows into it, the system learns from the best coordinator on the planet. That turns into a human-in-the-loop stage where the system becomes more autonomous and Krista has less and less manual coordination work to do. Then the next domino: NEPC staff. Then the final domino: the players.

## Why Krista First

Krista holds a social map of five hundred players in her memory. Who clicks with whom. Who clashes. Which combinations create magic on a Tuesday evening. Which player needs encouragement after a rough week. She makes sixty-plus group composition decisions every single week and she is almost always right.

That judgment is the most valuable training data in the company. Every decision Krista makes is a labeled example of what a great coordinator does. If we can capture her decisions, her corrections, and her reasoning, we have something no competitor can replicate. The AI does not need to be smarter than Krista. It needs to learn from Krista until it is good enough that she trusts it to handle the routine stuff while she focuses on the hard stuff.

## Phase 1: Krista's Agent

What Krista gets: A private Slack bot that is always available. She pours everything she knows into it. Player observations. Group chemistry notes. Who to keep apart. Who needs a confidence boost. The agent stores it all, encrypted, organized, and searchable.

What the system learns: Every time Krista builds a group through the Give 'n Take platform, the system records who she placed together and who she kept apart. When she edits an AI suggestion, the edit is training data. When she discards a suggestion entirely, that is training data too. Her corrections reveal what the algorithm is missing.

How Krista sees results: The system starts suggesting group compositions. At first, Krista edits most of them. But each edit makes the next suggestion better. The metric that matters is adoption rate. What percentage of AI suggestions does Krista use as-is, use with edits, or throw away? That number going up over time is the proof that the system is learning.

What Krista does not have to do: She does not have to fill out forms. She does not have to rate players. She does not have to learn new software. She talks to her agent the way she talks to a colleague. The agent extracts the structured signals from her natural language. She pours in messy human knowledge. The system organizes it.

The trust loop: Krista tries a suggestion. It works. She tries another. It works better. She starts trusting the easy ones and focusing her attention on the hard ones. The system handles the routine. Krista handles the edge cases. Her workload drops while quality stays the same or improves. That is the moment she becomes an evangelist for the system instead of a skeptic.

## Phase 2: Expert Staff Get Agents

Who: The pros, the coordinators, the front desk staff at NEPC. Anyone who interacts with players and has opinions about who works well together.

What they get: Their own Give 'n Take agent, similar to Krista's but scoped to their expertise. A teaching pro might focus on skill development observations. A front desk staffer might notice who arrives in groups, who looks for familiar faces, who seems out of place.

Why it matters: Krista is one person. She cannot be at every session. Expert staff see things she does not. A pro running a clinic notices that a player is way better than their comfort zone suggests. A coordinator notices two players who always arrive together but never get placed together. Each observation enriches the community graph.

The compounding effect: The AI now learns from multiple expert perspectives, not just one. Contradictions between experts surface interesting cases. When two experts disagree about a player, that disagreement is signal. The system gets smarter faster because it has more diverse training data.

## Phase 3: Every Player Gets Their Own Agent

What the player experiences: A personalized invitation feed. Sessions the agent thinks they will love, with the people it knows they want to play with. They RSVP yes or no. Every response teaches the agent. When they want to go deeper, they chat directly with their agent through an encrypted channel. They tell it who they loved playing with, who drained them, what they want more of.

The privacy unlock: Because privacy is guaranteed by Sui cryptography, players can be brutally honest. I do not want to play with Mike. Sarah is the only reason I come on Tuesdays. That group makes me dread Wednesday. Every player thinks these things. Nobody says them. Give 'n Take makes it safe to say them. And the system coordinates around them without ever exposing them.

The adoption flywheel: A few players try it. They tell their agent the truth. They start getting noticeably better matches. Other players see the results and want in. Trust builds through experience, not persuasion. Adoption compounds because the experience is jaw-dropping.

## What This Demo Shows

This demo is a working prototype of Phase 3, the player agent experience. It runs on real Sui testnet infrastructure. The community graph, the invitation feed, the private encrypted conversation, the recent sessions, the clickable player interactions. Everything you see here is the future of how five hundred NEPC members, and eventually millions of players across thousands of clubs, will interact with their Give 'n Take agent.

But the product strategy starts with Phase 1. Krista first. Earn her trust. Let her intelligence train the system. Then expand outward. The player agent demo proves the vision is real. The Krista-first approach proves the path to get there is practical.

## The Big Picture

Get this working for Krista first. She pours everything in. The system learns from the best. Expand to expert staff. More diverse training data. Then give every player their own agent. The privacy guarantee unlocks data that no other system can access. The community graph compounds. Better matches. Deeper connections. More honest feedback. Smarter AI. The loop accelerates.

One club first. NEPC. Then ten clubs. Then a hundred. Every correction from every coordinator at every club trains the same system. The intelligence transfers. New clubs adopt a system that already understands how to build great groups, seeded by the patterns Krista taught it. The coordinator dependency dissolves as the intelligence scales.

This is not a technology demo. This is the first step toward measuring and powering real human value exchange in real-world communities. Starting with pickleball. Extending to every community built on human connection.
